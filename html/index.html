What is MR?

  • Mixed reality refers to environments where real and virtual objects interact seamlessly and in real-time, allowing users to interact with and manipulate both physical and digital objects at the same time.

How does MR interact with two different worlds (physical and virtual worlds)?

  • Track the user's body position in the physical world
  • spatial mapping and scene understanding technology
  • Awareness of ambient lighting and sound
  • recognize objects
  • Awareness of user's surrounding physical locations

MR vs AR vs VR

  • AR adds a layer of virtual objects to the real-world environment (no direct interaction between the virtual and physical world objects.)
  • VR is a fully immersive virtual environment (nothing is from the physical world. All are virtual objects created by computers.)
  • MR vs. AR—MR allows virtual and physical objects to interact in real-time, but AR can’t interact with virtual and physical objects.
  • MR vs VR—MR can have virtual and physical objects co-exist, but VR blocks all the physical world objects and replaces them with virtual objects.
